Assignment: Build a tower out of straws, tape, string, and a cup that can hold the most pennies in the cup without the cup touching the ground

Plan: The plan was to make a rectangular pyramid out of the straws, as this seemed to be the most resilient structure for weight-bearing. The cup would be tied to the center, with the intention to distribute the weight of the pennies onto all 4 supports of the pyramid.

Result: Our structure actually performed the worst, as the cup touched the ground after 2 pennies were placed in the cup.

Conclusion: Our original structure was actually one of the best ideas, but our problem was that the pyramid was too close to the ground, and therefore could only hold a few pennies. The structure was strong, as it used triangles as weight-bearers, but the elasticity of the string caused the cup to drop under light weight.

My Contribution: I suggested that we incorporate triangles because they are one of the best shape for weight bearing as they distribute the weight.

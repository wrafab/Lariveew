Bubble Lamp

Assignment: Build a "Lamp Shade" using the 3D printer

I had an idea in mind for a lamp when I started. I wanted to take a glass water bottle and put it into a 3D printed base that had colorful LEDS to light up the water in the bottle. I also wanted to add an air pump and some tubeing into the lamp to give the effect of bubbles in the water.

The first step was to make the base and top of the lamp. I had a water bottle that I thought would work, so I made the base to surround that. On the bottom base, I added some stands so I would have room to put the LEDS under the water bottle. I also added a hole in the back so I could route wire through to power the LEDS. I molded the top around the cap of the water bottle so I could screw it on. This made the lamp easy to open if needed and also saved me the time and frustration of trying to waterproof the plastic top. Finally, I hollowed out the top so I had room for a small air pump and again added a hole for wires. I also made a cap for the top to cover up the hollowed gap so it would look cleaner.

The printed parts turned out ok, but they were a bit smaller than I thought (or the bottle was bigger).After some heavy sanding, I finally got the parts to squeeze into place. I added the LEDS to the bottom base and routed the wire out the side hole. Instead of buying an air pump, I decided to try and make one using a bottle cap, a balloon, and a computer fan motor.

I secured the balloon tightly onto the bottle cap with a clamp and some super glue. I drilled 3 holes into the cap, 1 for the air tube and 2 for airflow. In front of the airflow holes I glued some rubber so the pumped air would be forced out the air tube instead of back out the holes. After some modifications to the motor, I glued a piece of metal hanger wire to the balloon and then to the motor. When the motor turns, the wire pushes and pulls the balloon in a pumping motion, pulling air in through the airflow holes and pushing it back out through the air tube.
